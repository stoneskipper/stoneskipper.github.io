The history and legend of Pixy Stoneskipper

I go by Pixy Stoneskipper. That isn't the name on my birth certificate, and yeah, it sounds fruity. The name is a good fit for who I am and how I feel. I've been influenced by artists and hobos and people with idiosyncrasies. I am inspired by people who are unwilling or unable to follow convention.

I chose the name Pixie as in "forest pixie" or "manic pixie dream girl." Then I changed the spelling because four letters is fewer than five. I chose the name Stoneskipper, because I am good at skipping stones. I've always liked the naming convention of earning a name later in life based on traits or heroic acts.

When I was in my 20's, before taking a new name, I was a bicycle mechanic. Bicycles were all I thought about. I learned everything I could about building and repairing bicycles. I felt deep satisfaction every time I learned a new fact or technique that I could add to what I know. Eventually, after many years, I felt satisfied with the volume of information and confidence I'd acquired - I was certain that I could assemble and repair a bicycle that I could rely on forever. I had transportation solved. I knew that for the remainder of my life I would never be stuck somewhere, and I would be able to move and travel freely at a pace of up to 100 miles per day if necessary.

My loving relationship with bicycles and my need to understand them was as practical and logical as it was romantic. The bicycle: here was a machine that you could acquire for free, which could transport you speedily for infinite distances. It requires no license or permission. If you can operate and repair a bicycle, you are free at the most fundamental level. As an almost unnecessary bonus, you will feel exhilaration as you become stronger every time you ride.
